Standard RAG retrieves text chunks independently and loses the relational structure that governs how concepts connect. GRAG instead retrieves from a graph: a divide-and-conquer subgraph retrieval algorithm scores the top-k subgraphs in linear time, and a dual text-view/graph-view prompt gives the LLM both the natural-language context and an explicit structural view. The method outperforms SOTA RAG baselines on multi-hop graph reasoning benchmarks.
