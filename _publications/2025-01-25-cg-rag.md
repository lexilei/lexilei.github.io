Scientific QA requires tracing ideas across papers via citations, but standard chunk retrieval ignores that structure. CG-RAG builds a chunk-level contextual citation graph and introduces Lexical-Semantic Graph Retrieval (LeSeGR), which fuses sparse (lexical) and dense (semantic) signals through graph message passing rather than late-stage score combination. We show theoretically that existing hybrid retrieval methods fall out as a special case of LeSeGR, and empirically that the approach improves multi-document scientific QA.
